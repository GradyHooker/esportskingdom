League of Legends Tier Rankings as of 8 November 2020

We are back with a special edition of our League of Legends tier rankings. Since Worlds 2020 showed us the first glimpse of cross-region competition we have brought together our rankings into a big one-off World Tier Rankings!

Teams that did not qualify for Worlds 2020 are placed based on our best approximation with regards to how the other teams in their region performed. To distinguish them, all teams that reached the Main Event of Worlds 2020 have a little icon in the top left of their box.

Tier Rankings compiled by Ryan "Hullitude" Hull (@Hulltude on Twitter) and Grady Hooker (@GradyHooker on Twitter).

7 September 2020 (Summer Seasons 2020)

The road to Worlds is coming to an end as all of the seasons wrap up. We have a bunch of national champions from their regions and a bunch of other teams that have also squeezed through to Worlds - but how do they all stack up?

12 July 2020 (Mid-Season Summer 2020)

We are roughly halfway into the Summer season for the LCK, LPL, LEC, and LCS. That means it is time for Grady and Ryan to put their heads together and come up with another edition of the Tier Rankings to put the teams where they belong!

17 May 2020 (Spring Seasons 2020)

With the conclusion of all the Spring Seasons in league of Legends, it is time for another Tier Rankings. Since esports is moving to a more regional-based system and the Mid-Season Invitational wont be happening this year, we have ranked each of the LCK, LEC, LPL, and LCS individually.

5 March 2020 (Middle of Spring Seasons)

We are roughly halfway through most of the Spring seasons in the League of Legends leagues. So it is time for Ryan and Grady to get together once again and try to collate all of the teams together into a tier rankings. Unfortunately because of the lack of play, we have had to skip LPL for this edition.

12 November 2019 (2019 World Championship)

What better time to start up our Esports Tier Rankings for a second game, League of Legends, than right after one of the biggest esports tournaments of the year - Worlds 2019. Thanks to Ryan "Hullitude" Hull we attempt to take the top teams from all of the regions and sort them into tiers.
